Bootcamp Basics: Have Fun With Your Project Group!

Posted January 25, 2019

The first project week of Bootcamp. I am pretty sure that phrase is supposed to strike fear into the hearts of all. However, looking back on project week, I have to say that contentment and pride is what lives in my heart. Is our project perfect? Of course not. Were there problems we faced? Absolutely. Am I most proud of the product or of the team that produced it? 100% the TEAM!

Today's quote from the API call to They Said So for an inspirational quote of the day (used in our project) got me thinking about the special qualities of each member of the team I am priviledged to call myself a part of:

We are each gifted in a unique and important way. It is our privilege and our adventure to discover our own special light.
-Mary Dunbar

It has been my privilege and adventure to discover the amazing qualities of my teammates. Each of the members of our team has very special talents. Billy has tons of IT industry experience and brings the level-headed calm of one who knows that minor hiccups are the norm rather than the rule. A COBOL programmer originally, he brings the wise counsel of experience and a calming presence for those of us who might be more inclined to panic (or throw keyboards). Jodi, with experience in graphic design and architecture, knows more about choosing colors and designing a beautiful product than I can ever hope to learn (but I am, of course, taking notes!). Running a business with her husband has taught her the subtle art of time management and prioritizing so that even if the bells and whistles are not attached yet, the MVP is great. I am more the "ideas" girl. The idea for the project came from my real-world struggles to collect data efficiently in my classroom. While I am the newest of the bunch to the tech field, I am proud of what I have learned so far in the Bootcamp and of the meaningful contributions I made to the project.

The thing that makes our team really stand out, though? It is how much FUN we have! We used an Agile project mangagement approach during project week, and got so into the habit of daily standups at 5:00 that we decided to keep doing them even after project week finished. We are in an online Bootcamp and one might think that could prevent teams from bonding. That might be the case for other teams, but not for us! Our instructor commented after our presentation that she thought our team definitely had the most fun.

    A few ideas for "virtual" team fun:
  • Make sure everyone's strengths are utilized and acknowledged- nothing builds a team better than everyone knowing their contribution is important!
  • Photoshop crowns onto screenshots of your teammates and give titles like "King of Modals" or "Queen of Design"
  • Have a Zoom Room dance party- take screenshots and share them on Slack

The most important thing that I learned during the first project week at Bootcamp is that things WILL go wrong, difficulties WILL arise, and you WILL begin to feel overwhelmed. There will be a day when everything goes wrong and you want to curl up into a ball and hide from the code. That is when you will need your team to remind you that you are in this thing together and will make it through together- and a good team will help you to look back and only remember the dance party.
