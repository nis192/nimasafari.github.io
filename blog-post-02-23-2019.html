Why Hire a Bootcamp Grad?

Posted February 23, 2019

Having just cleared the halfway point in GA Tech's Full Stack Web Development Bootcamp, prepping for the upcoming job hunt has started in earnest. Prior to enrolling, I did some research on whether or not Bootcamp grads are marketable. While there is much debate on the subject, in the end, after weighing all of the options, I chose a Bootcamp program. It may not be the right decision for everyone, but I believe it was for me. So why should companies seriously consider hiring Bootcamp graduates like me?

In Bootcamp You Learn... FAST

Do you need someone who can learn a new technology efficiently? Hire a Bootcamp grad. In a Bootcamp, you learn to quickly incorporate new learning to make effective real-world products. I can think of only a few rare moments in so far when concepts were taught outside of the context of a problem likely to be encountered in the real world. Utilizing the same resources available to developers in industry is encouraged and, at least in Trilogy Bootcamps, the main projects are all team projects and groups are encouraged to utilize Agile project management practices. Bootcamps are designed to teach students the strategies to work efficiently and solve problems in the same manner as they will on the job. Technology changes rapidly - employers need people who can evolve with it.

Bootcampers Generally Have Real-World Experience

I cannot stress this enough. With very few exceptions, my Bootcamp peers and I are college graduates who are changing careers entirely or have goals to level up from current positions in the field. We have much more to offer than just knowledge. We have experienced the "real world" of work and have developed the soft skills that many employers are desperate for. While many students have diverse technical skills, the value of a person with 10+ years teaching experience in terms of ability to communicate, learn efficiently, and understand team dynamics cannot be understated. Bootcamp grads generally bring to the table much more than just "what I learned in Bootcamp" - they bring life experience and interdisciplinary knowledge as well.

Bootcamp Grads Want to Continue to Learn

We are a diverse group of people from all over the world, but the one thing that unites everyone in my cohort is that we all crave to know more. We dive deeply into the content. Bootcampers, in my experience, choose this path in order to learn and grow not only efficiently but effectively. We may have different long-term goals, but we all want to continue to grow and develop our skills.

Are Bootcamps a perfect solution to the problem of an ever-increasing need for great developers? Of course not. They are a quick way to get started, but should never be considered the end. Upon graduating from my Bootcamp experience I am confident that I will be a valuable asset to any company, but I am fully aware that I won't know everything (who does?) and will need to continue to develop my skills. Do I have a plan for that? Absolutely!
